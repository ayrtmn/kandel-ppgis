Welcome to Kandel

A negotiation game about solar panels, farmland, and the future of energy

📍 The Setting

Kandel is a small town in Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany — at the heart of one of Europe's largest solar energy debates. In 2022, a proposed freiflächenphotovoltaik (open-space PV) installation sparked a regional controversy: hundreds of hectares of agricultural land potentially converted to solar.

This game uses a fictional 50-hex map of Kandel to simulate the trade-offs four stakeholder groups face when deciding where solar panels should — or should not — go.

🎮 How to Play
1
Round 1 — Restrictions

All groups vote on all 50 hexes. Restrict a hex to protect it from PV. If no one prefers it, all groups that care about that land earn protection points.

2
Round 2 — Preferences

Vote again. Prefer a hex to build PV there. If built, all groups that care about that land lose impact points.

3
Round 3 — Conflict Resolution

Hexes with both restrict and prefer votes go to negotiation. No automatic scoring — discuss, trade, and decide.
